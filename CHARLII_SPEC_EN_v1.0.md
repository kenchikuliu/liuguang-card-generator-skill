# 1. Fixed Structure: 6 Cards

## Card 1｜Cover Card (image-gen)

**Components:**

* AI-generated cover image
* title (max 2 lines)
* 2 short intro lines

**Rules:**

* no body text
* no bullet list
* no detailed explanation

**Goal:**
Grab attention and introduce the core judgment.

---

## Card 2｜Problem Card

**Purpose:**
Make the reader feel:
"This is exactly my problem."

**Structure:**

* relatable situation
* visible pain point
* common mistake
* transition to the core idea

---

## Card 3｜Conclusion Card

**Purpose:**
Give the answer early.

**Structure:**

* one clear judgment
* a 3-part framework
* one punchline

---

## Card 4｜Path A

## Card 5｜Path B

**Structure:**

* who it is for

**Purpose:**
Elevate the idea and reinforce your point of view.

**Structure:**

# 2. Core Writing Style

## Definition

**Opinion-led card writing**

This is not explanation-first writing.
It is judgment-first writing.

---

## 5 Core Principles

1. Lead with the conclusion
2. One line, one idea
3. Break information into blocks
4. Isolate the strongest line
5. One card, one core point

---

## Example

❌ Article style:
Many people are inefficient not because they lack ability, but because they choose the wrong tools.

✅ Card style:
Most people are not slow
because they lack ability.

They are slow
because they picked the wrong tools.

---

# 3. Sentence Splitting Rules

## 1) One sentence should carry only one idea

Split when any of these happens:

* more than 12 English words
* multiple commas
* multiple actions in one sentence
* contains turning words like:
  * because
  * but
  * so
  * for example
  * however

---

## 2) Parallel ideas must become a list

❌ Wrong:
It supports logging in, clicking buttons, waiting for content to load, and then taking screenshots.

✅ Better:
It can:

* log in
* click buttons
* wait for content to load
* take screenshots

---

## 3) Long blocks must be broken up

Rule:

👉 Every 2–3 lines, create a new block.

English cards need visible rhythm.
Without rhythm, they feel like mini paragraphs.

---

# 4. English-Specific Layout Rules

This is the most important upgrade.

English cards should not follow Chinese layout rules directly.

---

## 1) Never break a word across lines

❌ Not allowed:

* automa / tion
* work / flow
* OpenC / law

A word must stay intact.

---

## 2) Protect key terms as a whole

These should stay together whenever possible:

* product names
* platform names
* API names
* workflow terms
* common technical phrases

Examples:

* OpenClaw
* workflow automation
* content aggregation
* screenshot API

---

## 3) English should be written in shorter thought units

Chinese can tolerate denser lines.
English cannot.

❌ Weak:
Having more tools does not necessarily mean that you will become more efficient.

✅ Better:
More tools
do not mean
more efficiency.

---

## 4) Use punchline rhythm

English cards work best when they sound sharp.

Good rhythm looks like this:

More tools.
More noise.

Not more output.

Or:

The problem is not the tool.
The problem is the choice.

---

## 5) English needs rewriting, not translation

A direct translation often sounds flat.
For English cards:

* shorten harder
* remove filler
* make it sound native
* prioritize force over completeness

---

# 5. Layout Rules for Overflow Control

## Word count is not enough

Use these as practical ranges:

| Range           | Word Count   |
| --------------- | ------------ |
| Safe            | 60–110 words |
| Comfortable max | 120 words    |
| Risk zone       | 130+ words   |

English expands visually faster than Chinese.

---

## Line rules

* max 10 body lines per card
* max 2 lines per block
* at least 2 visual breaks per card

---

## Single-line rules

* 4–8 words per line is ideal
* 10 words per line is the hard ceiling
* anything longer should usually be split

---

## Structure rules

Each card should contain:

* 4–6 information blocks
* 1 core takeaway
* optional list with max 3 items

---

# 6. Overflow Engine for English Cards

## Check order

### Step 1｜Check word count

If too long, cut content first.

### Step 2｜Check line count

If over 10 lines, split and compress.

### Step 3｜Check sentence rhythm

If a line feels like a paragraph, rewrite it.

### Step 4｜Check term integrity

If a key term is broken or squeezed, isolate it.

---

## Compression order

Always compress in this order:

1. remove filler
2. shorten long clauses
3. turn parallel ideas into bullets
4. move the conclusion forward
5. only then reduce line length further

---

## Common filler to remove in English

* in many cases
* it is important to note that
* you need to understand that
* in order to
* this can help you
* one of the key things is
* in other words

---

# 8. Blog → Card Workflow

## Step 1｜Extract the core thesis

Use this format:

This piece is not really about X.
It is about how to judge Y.

---

## Step 2｜Force a 3-part structure

Whenever possible, turn the content into:

* 3 paths
* 3 levels
* 3 mistakes
* 3 decisions

English cards benefit a lot from this.

---

## Step 3｜Assign each card one clear job

| Card   | Job          |
| ------ | ------------ |
| Card 1 | hook         |
| Card 2 | relatability |
| Card 3 | answer       |
| Card 4 | path A       |
| Card 5 | path B       |
| Card 6 | elevation    |

---

## Step 4｜Rewrite into card language

Not shorter.
Rebuilt.

English cards are not condensed articles.
They are structured judgments.

# 10. Final Test

Use this one question to test every English card:

**Is this card delivering information,
or helping the reader make a judgment?**

If it only delivers information,
it will feel weak.

If it helps the reader decide,
it will travel further.

# 11. English Enhancement Layer

## A. Prefer contrast

English card writing becomes stronger when it uses contrast.

Examples:

* not more tools, but better choices
* not more input, but better filtering
* not faster action, but better judgment

---

## B. Prefer punchlines over explanations

Instead of:

This means that you should be more intentional when selecting tools.

Use:

Choose slower.
Work better.

Or:

Better tools matter.
Better judgment matters more.

---

## C. Prefer verbs over abstractions

❌ Weak:
efficiency improvement through optimized selection

✅ Better:
choose better
work faster
waste less

---

## D. Prefer native rhythm

English cards should sound like something a person would actually post.

Not like translated documentation.
Not like formal essays.
Not like slides copied from an article.

---

# 12. Card Writing Formula

## Card Formula

**Hook**
A relatable tension

**Shift**
What people think is wrong

**Judgment**
What is actually true

**Framework**
2–3 clear paths or choices

**Punchline**
A line worth remembering
